How to Make Terrible Decisions About Cybersecurity

Why Would Anyone Listen to Me?

I wouldn't. I've been working on being successful in cybersecurity for the past four years. Spent eight years in the Army and then used my GI bill to get a master's degree in cybersecurity. I'm actively working on following a five year plan that Black Hills Information Security's John Strand wrote. This led me to pursue multiple certifications and this course through General Assembly. See the five year plan link below for more info. I'm really interested in the mechanics of the internet but am staying far away from Computer Science.

Training Resources

Here is a list of solid training resources I've accumulated over the past four years.

The OSI model is a pain in the ass but it's definitely been helpful for me to break down areas that I need to study. From my time in the military I was extremely interested in Routing and Switching. That led me to work on CCNA from Cisco which I earned at the beginning of 2021. I'm hoping to go for CCNP security some time in the next couple years. Check out the routing and switching portion of John Strand's 5 year plan for some great ideas about getting started.

Sweet Conferences

Some cool opportunities out there to get out and get belly to belly while learning some sweet skills.

  • Wild West Hackin Fest

    One of the rare affordable vendors that is well versed in real world applications.

  • B Sides

    One of the best ways to meet folks in the industry in your area. There is not a better networking opportunity out there.

  • SANS

    Hope you have a training budget! Urban legend is that they'll give you a discount if you help set up. The cadillac of conferences and most coveted by employers.
